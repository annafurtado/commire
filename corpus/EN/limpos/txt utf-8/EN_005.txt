Guide for asylum seekers in Québec wishing to obtain Québec government services

Important - Register for an information session by calling 514 271-3533

AN INFORMATION SESSION FOR YOU!

You are living in Québec while your application for asylum is being reviewed. The Government of Québec offers various services related to accommodation, financial assistance, health care and schooling for your children.

To obtain information on these services and practical advice on life in Québec, register for an information session given by La Maisonnee, a partner organization of the Ministère de l'immigration, de la Diversité et de l'inclusion.

The sessions are given in French, Spanish and English.
Call 514 271-3533 for information.
Being informed is important! This document will serve as a checklist.

THE COMMON VALUES OF Québec SOCIETY
Like many other societies, Québec has adopted values that establish ways of living together, behaving in society and making collective decisions. Knowing and respecting these values will make your life easier.
In Québec:
Speaking French is a necessity;
Women and men have the same rights;
Political and religious powers are separate;
The exercise of human rights and freedoms must respect the rights and freedoms of others and the general well-being.

Québec is:
a free and democratic society;
a society based on the rule of law;
a society enriched by its diversity.

To learn more, consult the site www.immigration-Québec.gouv.qc.ca/common-values

THE SERVICES OF A CONSULTANT
If you use the services of a consultant, make sure that the consultant is an authorized person [1]. In Québec, a person authorized to represent you can be a lawyer who is a member of the Barreau du Québec or a notary who is a member of the Chambre des notaires du Québec. Ask to see the person’s membership card.

You may also retain the services of a member in good standing of the Immigration Consultants of Canada Regulatory Council. Refer to www.iccrc-crcic.ca for the list of members.

Important - If you use the services of a consultant who is not a lawyer who is a member of the Barreau du Québec, his or her fees will not be reimbursed.

SCHOOLS AND FRENCH LANGUAGE COURSES
School for children
You can register your children for school. Access is free for pre-schoolers (5 years of age), elementary school children, (6 to 12 years of age) and secondary school children (12 to 18 years of age). Some schools offer preschool education to 4-year-old children.
For information on registering your child for school, you can contact the Ministère de I ’Education et de I’Enseignement superieur at 418 643-7095 or at 1 866 747-6626 (toll free) or consult the website at www.education.gouv.qc.ca.

Important - You must submit your Refugee Protection Claimant Document issued by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada.

School for adults
If you are over 18 years of age and wish to study, you must:
pay the tuition fees charged for international students;
obtain a Québec Acceptance Certificate (Certificat d’acceptation du Québec) issued by the Ministère de l’immigration, de la Diversité et de l'inclusion. For more information, call 514 864-9191 or 1 877 864-9191 (toll free).
obtain a study permit issued by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada. For information, call 1 888 242-2100 (toll free).

French language courses for adults
It is not necessary to obtain a study permit or a Québec Acceptance Certificate (Certificat d’acceptation du Québec) in order to take French language courses. The courses are free of charge but registration fees may apply.
To obtain the contact information for partner organizations of the Ministère de l'immigration, de la Diversité et de l'inclusion that offer part-time French courses, call 514 864-9191 or 1 877 864-9191 (toll free)
To find a partner organization that offers French courses, visit the following address: www.Frenchspellssuccess.gouv.qc.ca and click on the registration schedule.
To obtain the contact information for adult education centres that offer full-time and part-time French courses, call the Ministère de I ’Education et de I’Enseignement superieur at 418 643-7095 or 1 866 747-6626 (toll free).

HEALTH CARE AND SOCIAL SERVICES
Some health care services and prescription drugs are covered by the Interim Federal Health Program or the lRégie de l’assurance maladie du Québec.
To access these services and medications, you must present certain documents.
If you are receiving last-resort financial assistance, you always need to show the doctor or pharmacist:
your Carnet de réclamation (claim card) issued by the Ministère du Travail, de l'Emploi et de la Solidarité sociale;
your Refugee Protection Claimant Document or
your Interim Federal Health Program Certificate issued by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada or the Canada Border Services Agency.
If you are not receiving last-resort financial assistance (because you work, for example), you must obtain the temporary document entitled Confirmation de votre couverture pour les médicaments d’ordonnance fournis au Québec (confirmation of your coverage for prescription drugs provided in Québec).
To learn the procedure to follow to obtain this confirmation, call the general information line of the Régie de l’assurance maladie du Québec at 1 800 561-9749 (toll free).

Subsequently, you must always show the doctor or pharmacist:
your Confirmation de votre couverture pour les médicaments d’ordonnance fournis au Québec (confirmation of your coverage for prescription drugs provided in Québec);
your Refugee Protection Claimant Document or your Interim Federal Health Program Certificate

Where to receive health care

If you have health problems related to your situation as a refugee claimant, you can access the Programme régional d’accueil et d’intégration des demandeurs d’asile (régional refugee claimant reception and intégration program) of the CIUSSS du Centre-Ouest-de-I’Tle-de-Montréal. You can go to the CIUSSS with or without an appointment.

The Info-Santé service gives you access by telephone to a health care professional for non-emergency problems. It is available 24/7 and can answer routine health care questions or refer you to the appropriate resource in the health and social services network. Call 811 from anywhere in Québec.
When an urgent health problem arises, you must go to an emergency department (medical clinic, hospital) as quickly as possible or dial 911 to obtain help (police, ambulance, fire department).

THE PROTECTION OF PERSONAL INFORMATION

Your personal information is confidential and may not be disclosed without your consent. However, the Ministère de l’immigration, de la Diversité et de l'inclusion may, without your consent:

communicate personal information to Canadian immigration authorities and Québec public agencies if doing so is necessary to enforce a Québec statute;

be authorized to disclose personal information to Canadian immigration authorities or to public Québec organizations if this disclosure is necessary in the exercise of their powers, the implementation of a program that they manage, or in providing services that are offered by the Ministère, in particular for identification purposes, or when the disclosure is clearly made for your benefit or for your safety in emergency situations.

The Ministère may, under provisions in the Act respecting Access to documents held by public bodies and the Protection of personal information (chapter A-2.1), transm it certain personal information concerning you to government departments and bodies. Specifically, such information may concern your address or information on your legal status as a refugee claimant in Québec that will enable them to ascertain whether you are still eligible for certain government services.

Access to this information is strictly limited to those individuals authorized to examine it pursuant to the Act.

To learn what information about you is in the Ministère’s possession, you must send a written request to the ministerial official responsible for the protection of personal information, at the following address:

Secrétariat général
Ministère de l’immigration, de la Diversité et de l'inclusion
500, Boulevard Rene-Levesque Ouest
Montréal (Québec) H2Z1W7
